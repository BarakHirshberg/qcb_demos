Harmonic oscillator  (1-D)

A particle in a parabolic well V(x) = ½μω²x². The eigenstates are ψn(x) = Nn Hn(√α x) e−αx²/2 (Hermite polynomials, α = μω/ℏ), with equally-spaced energies En = (n+½)ℏω. Lengths are in units of √(ℏ/μω) (so α = 1) and energies in units of ℏω. Choose the quantum number n.

Energy levels in the well  — V(x) = ½μω²x²,   En = (n+½)ℏω

Equally-spaced levels (ΔE = ℏω) sit in the parabolic well; the zero-point energy is E0 = ½ℏω. The selected ψn(x) is drawn on its own energy line, extending past the classical turning points (dashed) into the classically forbidden region.
