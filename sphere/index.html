Particle on a sphere  (rigid rotor)

A particle free to move on the surface of a sphere. The Hamiltonian is purely rotational, so the eigenfunctions are the spherical harmonics ψℓm(ϑ,φ) = Yℓm(ϑ,φ) — the angular part of the hydrogen atom, with no radial part. Energies depend only on ℓ: Eℓ = ℏ²ℓ(ℓ+1)/2I, each (2ℓ+1)-fold degenerate. Choose ℓ and m = −ℓ…+ℓ.

Energy levels  Eℓ = ℏ²ℓ(ℓ+1)/2I  — rotational ladder & degeneracy

The energy depends only on ℓ. Unlike the hydrogen atom, levels spread apart as ℓ grows (∝ ℓ(ℓ+1)) and there is no upper bound. The selected level is divided into its 2ℓ+1 degenerate m-states (one segment each, labeled by m); the selected state is circled.
