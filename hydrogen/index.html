Hydrogen atom

The bound-state wavefunctions separate as ψnℓm(r,ϑ,φ) = Rnℓ(r) · Yℓm(ϑ,φ). Choose the quantum numbers — only the physically allowed values are offered: ℓ = 0…n−1 and m = −ℓ…+ℓ. Lengths are in units of the Bohr radius a (a = 1), nuclear charge Z = 1.

Energy levels  En = −1/2n²  — Coulomb well & degeneracy

The energy depends only on n. The selected level is split into its n² degenerate states — one segment per state, grouped by ℓ (s, p, d, …) with 2ℓ+1 values of m each (colors = ℓ, selected state circled); the other levels are plain lines. Levels get shallower and denser as they converge to the ionization limit E = 0.

Radial part  Rnℓ(r)

Normalized Laguerre function. Radial nodes: n − ℓ − 1.

Rnℓ(r) — amplitude

P(r) = r²|Rnℓ|² — radial probability density
